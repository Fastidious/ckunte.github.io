Snippets for Vim

21W17

It’s miserable to doom-scroll. And yet hard to turn away from reading about the unfolding human catastrophe, as graphs climb. The tragedy of my compatriots dying reminds me of my own privilege that is hard to reconcile with. Now that the world is reacting with alarm, and sending the much needed help, hope to see better days.

I wonder if I will ever see the tide turn towards science, and efforts towards strengthening public infrastructure. Away from religious zealotry, casteism, quackery, and authoritarianism operating in disguise.

Occasionally I set aside my traditional set-up in favour of a humbler one. For all last week, I hooked-up my Raspberry Pi 3B+ to an external display and did everything I do. It was a distraction that offered much relief. The starkness of a simpler system without privileged software was immediately obvious. That in the first few hours I almost gave up. Vim is incredibly feature-rich, if only one can map and muscle-train to fire the right shortcuts and keys.

Inspiration, as they say, can come from anywhere. Mine was from Gilles Castel, a student of mathematics at the University of Leuven. If his disarming post about how he takes notes in mathematics lectures using LaTeX and Vim will not make you sweat, then I do not know what will. It was fun to try, fail, try-again, and half-replicate what he does. The result is a collection of LaTeX snippets for Vim created for my own use.1 Using this, I can almost be as productive in Vim as I am in Sublime Text in writing technical reports. Here’s a demo of snippets in action.

If the video feels slow, then it is because I type slowly. The modal editing is not yet hard-wired in me to be able to earn a belt, but I am getting better. One day with practice. None of this was possible without Holger Rapp’s UltiSnips and Junegunn Choi’s vim-plug. I am grateful to the three Vim masters.
