Another death. My cousin widowed, and their five year old orphaned. I was at their engagement fourteen years ago.

The animal is tired. The mind does not know how to react. Instead, it chooses to work — to power through deliverables, code, models, and computations. Arms, legs, and the back ache hesitatingly, can’t be sure if it is the inside temperature. The body has its way of dealing with things. In between, there’s me in blur.

We watched Tehran this week. Wife and I both liked. It was part cultural experience, and part curiosity.

Perth mandated wearing masks in public. This was following a handful of infections in the city. It got relaxed last Saturday. So mask-on, mask-off, mask back-on is our new Tai chi routine.

Concerned by the terrible situation in India, Australia threatened its returning citizens with jail term + fines. Then, from news earlier today, that ‘Australia will remain locked off to the rest of the world until at least 2022’.

Speaking of bikes, my year-old features a cheap Shimano Tourney gear-set, and I spent the last evening thinking about replacing it. SRAM’s NX Eagle set is the one I’m keen on, but aside from being unsure if the bike’s rear hub (135×5 QR) has sufficient room to swap a 1×7 with a 1×12 drive train, the set is as expensive as the entire bike itself!

The language syntax for SACS, I released last December, crossed 200 downloads this week. For a little grind outside work, I had no idea many would be interested in something like this.

The other day over coffee, we discussed LATEX type-setting system. I offered to help set-up, to which my younger one said, “Best if you prepare a study plan first, as you can be quite repetitive.” The nerve! 😊
